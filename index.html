about the text

This site was designed by Laurel Schwulst and displays the current phase of the 72 microseasons (kō) of Japan, each lasting five days. Also called 72 pentads, they originated in China and were adapted to better match the seasonality of Japan in the Edo period. The current version is from the abbreviated traditional calendar of 1874.

The bottom center of the screen displays the Japanese kō. Descriptions of each from the wikipedia entry were converted to English via Google translate, then further edited by Laurel. Additional language versions are based on Google-translated results of the edited English phrases. Please reach out if you’d like to contribute better translations.

about the font

The typeface used is Pentameter VF, an expressive monospace with variable axes across width (wdth: 75–125), weight (wght: 100–700) and slant (slnt: 0–16), designed by Marie Otsuka and released at Occupant Fonts in 2021. As a typeface that celebrates the creative agency of hand-coding, its syncopated rhythm merges rigid monolinear mechanics with an exaggerated italic skeleton. It is paired with Morisawa’s Cinema Letter for the Japanese characters.

The variation settings for each phrase is set to a Width of 100, a Slant of 10, and a Weight that corresponds to each microseason, ranging from the minimum of 100 for the coldest days of the year, to the maximum of 700 for the hottest days.

Read more about the design process of Pentameter, view a PDF Specimen or font details, and license it at Type Network.
