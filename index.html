about the text

This site was designed by Laurel Schwulst and displays the current phase of the 72 microseasons (kō) of Japan, each lasting five days. Also called 72 pentads, they originated in China and were adapted to better match the seasonality of Japan in the Edo period. The current version is from the abbreviated traditional calendar of 1874.

The bottom center of the screen displays the Japanese kō. Descriptions of each from the wikipedia entry were converted to English via Google translate, then further edited by Laurel. Additional language versions are based on Google-translated results of the edited English phrases. Please reach out if you’d like to contribute better translations.

about the sound

The sounds were composed by Tiger Dingsun. Each sound loop was composed by taking the entire corpus of the kanji used in the names of the 72 microseasons, sorting them into categories (i.e. crops, flowers, birds, weather), and then assigning each category a particular instrument. Each microseason, which has a name 3-4 kanji long, thus corresponds with a loop that consists of 3 or 4 layers. Each loop is a little more than 5 seconds, so that if one were to listen to the entire year, it would take 365 seconds.

about the font

The typeface used is Pentameter VF, an expressive monospace with variable axes across width (wdth: 75–125), weight (wght: 100–700) and slant (slnt: 0–16). It was designed by Marie Otsuka and released at Occupant Fonts in 2021. The variation settings for the microseasons is set to a Width of 100, a Slant of 10, and a Weight that corresponds to the season’s temperature, ranging from the minimum of 100 for the coldest days of the year, to the maximum of 700 for the hottest days.

As a typeface that celebrates the creative agency of hand-coding, Pentameter draws from the spirit of handwriting as an “upright italic.” Its syncopated rhythm merges rigid monolinear mechanics with an exaggerated tails. The Japanese characters are set in Morisawa’s Cinema Letter. You can read more about the design process of Pentameter, view a PDF Specimen or font details, and license it at Type Network.
