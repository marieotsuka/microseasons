about the [ text ]

This website displays the current phase of the 72 kō or microseasons of Japan, each lasting five days. Also called 72 pentads, they originated in China and was adapted to better match the seasonality of Japan in the Edo period by court astronomer Shibukawa Shunkai. The current version is from the abbreviated traditional calendar of 1874. Three pentads (about 15 days) make up a *sekki, and the bottom of the website displays whether we are in the beginning (*), middle (**), or end (***) of the 24 sekki within the year.

This site was designed by Laurel Schwulst. Japanese descriptions from the wikipedia article were converted to English via Google translate, then further edited by Laurel. Additional language versions are based on Google translated results of the edited English phrases.

about the [ font ]

The typeface used is Pentameter, an expressive monospace with variable axes across width (wdth: 75–125), weight (wght: 100–700) and slant (slnt: 0–16), designed by Marie Otsuka and released at Occupant Fonts in 2021. As a typeface that celebrates the creative agency in hand-coding, its syncopated rhythm merges rigid monolinear mechanics with an exaggerated skeleton of italic forms.

The variation settings for each phrase are set to a Width of 100, a Slant of 10, and a Weight that corresponds to each microseason, ranging from the minimum of 100 for the coldest days of the year, to the maximum of 700 for the hottest days.
